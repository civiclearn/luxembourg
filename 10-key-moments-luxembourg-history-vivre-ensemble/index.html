10 Key Moments in Luxembourg History for the Vivre-Ensemble Test

Not dates to memorise blindly, but moments that explain why Luxembourg is what it is today.

Why history matters for Vivre-Ensemble

The Vivre-Ensemble test does not expect encyclopedic knowledge. It checks whether you understand the major turning points that shaped Luxembourg’s identity, institutions, and values.

Prepare with the full training

1. The origins of Luxembourg (10th century)

Luxembourg began as a small medieval fortress around the year 963.

Its strategic location would define its destiny for centuries, placing it at the crossroads of major European powers.

2. Luxembourg under Burgundian and Habsburg rule

For long periods, Luxembourg was ruled by foreign dynasties.

These centuries explain why the country developed a strong sense of resilience and adaptation.

3. The Congress of Vienna (1815)

After the Napoleonic Wars, the Congress of Vienna reshaped Europe.

Luxembourg became a Grand Duchy and entered a new phase as a recognised political entity.

4. The Treaty of London (1867)

This treaty guaranteed Luxembourg’s neutrality and independence.

It also led to the dismantling of the fortress, transforming the country’s role in Europe.

5. The First World War (1914–1918)

Despite its neutrality, Luxembourg was occupied by Germany.

This experience raised questions about neutrality and national sovereignty.

6. The Second World War (1940–1945)

The Nazi occupation deeply marked Luxembourgish society.

Resistance, forced conscription, and repression remain central to collective memory.

7. The end of neutrality after 1945

After the war, Luxembourg abandoned strict neutrality.

It chose cooperation and security through international alliances.

8. European integration

Luxembourg became a founding member of what is now the European Union.

Europe offered stability, influence, and a new framework for cooperation.

9. Economic transformation in the late 20th century

The decline of the steel industry forced Luxembourg to reinvent its economy.

Finance, services, and innovation gradually replaced heavy industry.

10. Luxembourg in the 21st century

Today, Luxembourg is a multicultural, multilingual country with a strong social model.

Its history explains its openness, its institutions, and the idea of vivre-ensemble.

What you should remember for the test

The Vivre-Ensemble test focuses on understanding, not memorisation.

If you can explain how these moments shaped Luxembourg’s values and institutions, you are well prepared.
