The Burgundian and Habsburg Periods in Luxembourg History

A decisive era shaping Luxembourg’s political destiny, essential for Vivre-Ensemble.

Understand Luxembourg under foreign rule

These periods explain why Luxembourg was long governed by powerful dynasties.

Start your preparation

The end of the medieval dynasty

After the extinction of the original Luxembourg dynasty in the 15th century, the territory entered a new phase of its history. Luxembourg no longer belonged to a local ruling family.

Control over the territory passed to powerful European dynasties.

The Burgundian period

In the 15th century, Luxembourg came under the control of the Dukes of Burgundy. The Burgundian state was one of the most powerful political entities in Western Europe at the time.

Luxembourg became part of a larger territorial ensemble governed from outside its borders.

Integration into the Habsburg domains

After the fall of Burgundy, Luxembourg passed into the hands of the Habsburg dynasty. The Habsburgs ruled vast territories across Europe.

Luxembourg was incorporated into the Habsburg Netherlands, reinforcing its strategic military importance.

A territory under foreign administration

During both the Burgundian and Habsburg periods, Luxembourg was governed by representatives appointed by distant rulers.

Despite foreign rule, local traditions and institutions continued to exist and evolve.

Strategic importance of Luxembourg

Luxembourg’s strong fortifications made it a key military site. It was often involved in European conflicts between major powers.

This strategic role explains why Luxembourg remained highly contested for centuries.

Candidates should understand how external domination shaped Luxembourg’s political resilience.
