Civic Duties of Residents and Citizens in Luxembourg

Responsibilities that support democratic life and social cohesion.

Understand your civic responsibilities

Civic duties help ensure respect for the law and living together in Luxembourg.

Start your preparation

What are civic duties?

Civic duties are obligations that individuals have toward the community and the state.

They contribute to the functioning of democracy and the rule of law.

Respect for the law

All residents and citizens must respect Luxembourg’s laws and regulations.

This duty applies equally to nationals and foreign residents.

Tax obligations

Paying taxes and social contributions is a key civic duty.

Taxes finance public services such as education, healthcare, and infrastructure.

Participation in public life

Citizens are encouraged to participate in democratic life, for example through voting or civic engagement.

Residents may also take part in local initiatives and associations.

Jury duty and public responsibilities

Certain citizens may be called to serve on juries or fulfil other public duties as required by law.

These responsibilities support the justice system and public institutions.

Respect for others and social cohesion

Civic duties include respecting the rights, freedoms, and dignity of others.

This respect is essential in a diverse and multicultural society like Luxembourg.
