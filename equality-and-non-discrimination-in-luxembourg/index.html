Equality and Non-Discrimination in Luxembourg

Fundamental principles of the Luxembourgish democratic state.

Understand equality in Luxembourg

Equality before the law is a cornerstone of civic life.

The principle of equality before the law

In Luxembourg, all persons are equal before the law.

This means that everyone must be treated in the same way in comparable situations, without unjustified distinctions.

Non-discrimination as a legal obligation

Non-discrimination prohibits unequal treatment based on personal characteristics.

These principles apply to public authorities and, in many cases, to private actors as well.

Protected grounds

Luxembourg law and international conventions protect individuals against discrimination on grounds such as sex, origin, nationality, religion, disability, or sexual orientation.

These protections reflect European and international human rights standards.

Gender equality

Equality between women and men is a key objective of Luxembourg’s public policies.

It applies in areas such as employment, education, pay, and political participation.

Institutions and legal remedies

Individuals who believe they have been discriminated against may seek legal remedies.

Courts and specialised bodies are responsible for enforcing equality and non-discrimination rules.
