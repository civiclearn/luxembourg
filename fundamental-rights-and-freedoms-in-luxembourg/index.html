Fundamental Rights and Freedoms in Luxembourg

Core values you must understand for the Vivre-Ensemble test.

Know your rights and freedoms in Luxembourg

Fundamental rights are a central part of the Vivre-Ensemble programme.

The importance of fundamental rights

Fundamental rights and freedoms protect individuals against abuses of power and guarantee respect for human dignity. In Luxembourg, these rights form a core part of the democratic system.

For the Vivre-Ensemble test, candidates must understand both the nature of these rights and their role in everyday life.

Constitutional protection

Many fundamental rights and freedoms are guaranteed by the Luxembourg Constitution. These include equality before the law, personal liberty and respect for private life.

Constitutional protection ensures that these rights have the highest legal value within the national legal system.

Main fundamental freedoms

Luxembourg guarantees several key freedoms, such as freedom of expression, freedom of religion and freedom of assembly. These freedoms allow individuals to express opinions and participate actively in society.

They are essential to democratic debate and social cohesion.

Equality and non-discrimination

Equality before the law is a fundamental principle in Luxembourg. Discrimination based on origin, gender, religion or other personal characteristics is prohibited.

This principle reflects the values of tolerance and inclusion that underpin the concept of vivre-ensemble.

Limits to fundamental rights

Fundamental rights are not absolute. In certain situations, they may be limited by law in order to protect public order, security or the rights of others.

These limits must always be proportionate and justified.

Why this topic matters for the Vivre-Ensemble test

Test questions may focus on identifying fundamental rights and understanding their importance in a democratic society.

Candidates should be able to explain how rights and freedoms contribute to living together in Luxembourg.
