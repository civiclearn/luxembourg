From small state to central actor in the European project.

The post-war European context

After the Second World War, European countries sought new forms of cooperation to prevent future conflicts.

Economic integration and political dialogue were seen as tools to secure lasting peace.

Luxembourg as a founding member

Luxembourg was among the six founding countries of the European Communities in the 1950s.

Despite its small size, the country played an active role in shaping the European project.

Hosting European institutions

Luxembourg became one of the seats of European institutions, hosting key administrative and judicial bodies.

This strengthened its position as a central actor within the European framework.

Benefits of European integration

European integration contributed to Luxembourg’s economic growth and political stability.

It also reinforced the country’s international visibility and influence.

A core element of foreign policy

Participation in the European Union became a cornerstone of Luxembourg’s foreign and economic policy.

The country consistently supports deeper cooperation among member states.
