Luxembourg During the Second World War

Occupation, resistance, and a decisive break with neutrality.

Understand Luxembourg under Nazi occupation

This period profoundly shaped modern Luxembourg.

Start your preparation

Luxembourg on the eve of war

At the end of the 1930s, Luxembourg remained officially neutral, hoping to avoid involvement in another major conflict.

Despite rising tensions in Europe, the country had no army capable of defending its territory.

The German invasion of 1940

On 10 May 1940, German forces invaded Luxembourg as part of their western offensive.

The invasion violated Luxembourg’s neutrality and marked the beginning of a harsh occupation.

Nazi occupation and annexation

Unlike during the First World War, Luxembourg was directly annexed by Nazi Germany.

The occupying authorities attempted to erase Luxembourgish identity through forced Germanisation.

Resistance and repression

Many Luxembourgers resisted the occupation through underground movements, strikes, and acts of defiance.

The population also suffered from repression, deportations, and forced conscription into the German army.

The government in exile

The Grand Duchess and the Luxembourg government fled abroad and continued to represent the country from exile.

Luxembourg officially aligned itself with the Allied powers.

Liberation and consequences

Luxembourg was liberated in 1944–1945 by Allied forces.

The experience of occupation led to the abandonment of neutrality after the war.
