Luxembourg During the World Wars (1914–1945)

A crucial historical period to understand for the Vivre-Ensemble test.

Understand Luxembourg’s modern history

The World Wars deeply shaped Luxembourg’s identity and values.

Luxembourg and the First World War

At the outbreak of the First World War in 1914, Luxembourg was a neutral country. Despite this neutrality, German troops occupied the country in order to facilitate military operations.

Although Luxembourg was not a battlefield, the occupation affected daily life and challenged the country’s political independence.

Consequences of the First World War

After the war, Luxembourg reaffirmed its independence and neutrality. The conflict led to political debates about the country’s future and its place in Europe.

These discussions contributed to the gradual evolution of Luxembourg’s political system.

Luxembourg during the Second World War

In 1940, during the Second World War, Luxembourg was again invaded and occupied by Nazi Germany. This occupation was far more severe than during the First World War.

The Luxembourg population faced repression, forced conscription and attempts to suppress national identity.

Resistance and exile

During the occupation, acts of resistance took place within the country. The Luxembourg government went into exile and continued to represent the country abroad.

These efforts helped preserve Luxembourg’s sovereignty and international recognition.

Liberation and lessons learned

Luxembourg was liberated in 1944–1945. The experience of the Second World War had a profound impact on the country’s commitment to democracy, human rights and international cooperation.

After the war, Luxembourg became an active supporter of European integration.

Why this period matters for the Vivre-Ensemble test

The test Vivre-Ensemble includes questions on Luxembourg’s experiences during the World Wars and their consequences.

Candidates should understand how these events shaped the country’s modern values and political choices.
