Luxembourg’s Multilingualism: Languages and Integration

A defining feature of Luxembourgish society, essential for the Vivre-Ensemble test.

Understand languages and daily life in Luxembourg

Multilingualism as a national characteristic

Luxembourg is known for its multilingual character. Several languages are used in daily life, administration, education and the media.

This linguistic diversity reflects the country’s history, location and openness to the world.

The three main languages in Luxembourg

Luxembourg officially recognises three languages: Luxembourgish, French and German. Each language has a specific role in society.

Luxembourgish is the national language, while French and German are widely used in administration, legislation and education.

Languages in education and administration

The education system is multilingual from an early age, helping residents develop strong language skills. Public administration also operates in several languages.

This system allows communication with a diverse population and supports social inclusion.

Multilingualism and integration

Knowledge of languages plays an important role in integration. Learning Luxembourgish, in particular, helps residents participate more fully in social and cultural life.

Multilingualism encourages dialogue and mutual understanding between different communities.

Why multilingualism matters for the Vivre-Ensemble test

The test Vivre-Ensemble includes questions about the languages used in Luxembourg and their respective roles.

Candidates should understand how multilingualism contributes to living together in a diverse society.
