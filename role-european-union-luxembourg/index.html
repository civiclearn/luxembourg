Luxembourg and the European Union

Luxembourg is a founding member of the European Union and has played a key role in European integration.

Its size has not limited its influence within European institutions.

European institutions based in Luxembourg

Several important EU institutions are located in Luxembourg.

These include parts of the European Commission, the Court of Justice of the European Union, and other bodies.

The Court of Justice of the European Union

The Court of Justice of the European Union ensures the uniform interpretation and application of EU law.

Its presence reinforces Luxembourg’s role as a legal and institutional hub.

The impact of EU membership

EU membership influences legislation, economic policies, and citizens’ rights.

Luxembourg residents benefit from freedoms such as free movement and equal treatment within the EU.

Economic and social effects

The European Union contributes to economic growth, employment, and cooperation.

It also supports social, environmental, and regional development policies.
