The Steel Industry and Its Decline in Luxembourg

From industrial backbone to economic transition.

Understand Luxembourg’s industrial past

The steel industry shaped Luxembourg’s society for over a century.

Start your preparation

The rise of the steel industry

At the end of the 19th century, Luxembourg experienced rapid industrialisation driven by the exploitation of iron ore in the south of the country.

Steel production became the main engine of economic growth and employment.

Steel as the backbone of the economy

For decades, the steel industry dominated Luxembourg’s economy and exports.

Entire towns and communities developed around steel plants and mines.

Social and demographic impact

The industry attracted large numbers of foreign workers, contributing to immigration and population growth.

It also played a key role in shaping Luxembourg’s working-class culture and social movements.

The steel crisis of the 1970s

From the 1970s onwards, global competition and economic changes led to a severe crisis in the steel sector.

Production declined, jobs were lost, and many facilities closed.

Managing decline and economic transition

The Luxembourg state actively supported restructuring and worker retraining.

This period marked the transition towards a more diversified economy.

Why this topic matters for the Vivre-Ensemble test

The steel industry explains many aspects of Luxembourg’s social and economic development.

Candidates should understand how the decline of steel led to economic transformation.
