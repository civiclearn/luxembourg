A Timeline of Luxembourg History for the Vivre-Ensemble Test

A chronological overview of the key periods every candidate should know.

Origins and the Middle Ages

Luxembourg’s history traditionally begins in 963 with the acquisition of the Bock rock by Count Sigefroid.

During the Middle Ages, Luxembourg develops as a fortified territory of major strategic importance.

Foreign rule and European dynasties

From the 15th century onwards, Luxembourg is governed by powerful European dynasties, including the Burgundians and the Habsburgs.

This long period of foreign rule shapes Luxembourg’s political resilience.

1815 and the birth of the modern state

The Congress of Vienna in 1815 establishes Luxembourg as a Grand Duchy.

This moment marks the beginning of Luxembourg as a modern political entity.

Neutrality and the 19th century

In 1867, the Treaty of London guarantees Luxembourg’s independence and neutrality.

The dismantling of the fortress ends Luxembourg’s military role.

The World Wars

Despite neutrality, Luxembourg is occupied during both the First and Second World Wars.

The Second World War leads to a decisive break with neutrality.

Post-war reconstruction and European integration

After 1945, Luxembourg rebuilds its economy and abandons neutrality.

The country becomes a founding member of European integration in the 1950s.

Contemporary Luxembourg

Since the late 20th century, Luxembourg has transformed into a financial and international service centre.

Immigration and European integration play a central role in modern society.

Why this timeline matters for the Vivre-Ensemble test

The Vivre-Ensemble test evaluates candidates’ ability to place events within the correct historical context.

A clear timeline helps structure knowledge and avoid confusion during the exam.
